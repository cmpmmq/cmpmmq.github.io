I wanna compare Chinese culture with Western culture in the aspect of sports.

When it comes to Western sports, we regard it as intense. In terms of Chinese sports, we think of it as moderate.

But it is a stereotype, and there are more complex historical reason behind.

Before the concept of modern sports was created, ancient sports was aimed at training soldiers to raise the general performance of the army, while modern sports is intended to train athletes to pursue individual human limitation and as the Olympic slogan says, to become “faster, higher and stronger”.

Therefore, it matters who is the rule-makers of modern sports. Let’s trace back to 1896 when the first modern Olympics revived in Athens, Greece, Westerners transformed their traditional sports events into modern ones with more competitiveness, entertainment and viewing value. However, we Chinese people failed that transformation and our national traditional sports were left behind with less intensity.

Then here raised a question: what should we do?

In a macroscopic perspective, our nation should put more emphasis on cultural output. Not only should play others’ sports well, but it’s of greater significance to play our own sports well and let the world play our games.

For individuals, we can think outside of the box and don’t set limitations for ourselves. There are some Westerners fascinated with body building, but the other side of the Western society is the high obesity rate.

As for me, what are my labels? “Easterner, Chinese, Girl, Major in the Department of Chinese Language and Literature, FDU College Student” What may be people’s imagination of such a person? An Eastern girl who plays moderate sports or no sports at all and always holds a book in hand? But I am just myself. I am a competitive distance runner and cyclist. I run 800m in 2:43, 10,000m in 44:05. and run 200kms a month in total.

Anyway, to conclude, what I learn from sports and life is how to strike a balance between mass and individual identity, between WE and ME culture. The key is – to be proud of the group and be true to yourself.

Thank you!
